Talking about disadvantage
in public narratives

To examine how narratives about disadvantage are presented in the Australian media, a team of Monash University researchers analysed more than two hundred articles from 2020 and further explored whether these narratives differ across different types of disadvantaged groups, issues and publishers. The dataset was first human-coded ‚Äì meaning that experts analysed the articles to look for emerging themes, keywords and biases ‚Äì which may help in developing machine learning models to track public narratives using artificial intelligence.

How we collect data and where are they come from?

We searched the Factiva database of Australian news articles for articles related to disadvantage. For example, this article contains key words related to themes of disadvantage highlighted in different colours.

  • We searched Factiva using the same set of terms that were developed for the review (e.g. disadvantage* OR discrimination OR "access to resources" ‚Ä¶)
  • We included the most-read newspaper in every state and territory, plus The Australian (top national newspaper), Daily Telegraph (second in NSW) and Herald Sun (second in Vic)
  • We restricted the searches to Commentaries/Opinions Or Editorials published in 2020.

We already know that each color block represents the group or issue that the article is coded for, so let's count the total number of articles for each group and issue in all coded articles.

Through a qualitative thematic synthesis of all the group labels mentioned, we generated a list of 26 groups that captured all of those discussed as disadvantaged in the included articles. We organised these groups under seven broad categories: cultural minorities, gender and sexuality, age, socioeconomic status, health status, institutionalisation, and other.
