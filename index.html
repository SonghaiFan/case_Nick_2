Talking about disadvantage
in public narratives

To examine how narratives about disadvantage are presented in the Australian media, a team of Monash University researchers analysed more than two hundred articles from 2020 and further explored whether these narratives differ across different types of disadvantaged groups, issues and publishers. The dataset was first human-coded, which may help in developing machine learning models to track public narratives using artificial intelligence.

Opinion pieces will be the focus of this study because they: (1) are likely to contain more narrative content (as opposed to the factual content that is generally presented in news stories); (2) typically highlight salient issues that are the forefront of public debate (Golan, 2010); (3) can impact public opinion and voting behaviour (Dalton et al., 1998; Druckman & Parkin, 2005; (4) are available in volumes that¬† are more amenable to human coding than other larger datasets, such as factual news stories; and, (5) were considered to be high-priority by the expert advisory panel.
