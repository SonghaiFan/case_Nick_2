Talking about disadvantage
in public narratives

To examine how narratives about disadvantage are presented in the Australian media, a team of Monash University researchers analysed more than two hundred articles from 2020 and further explored whether these narratives differ across different types of disadvantaged groups, issues and publishers. The dataset was first human-coded, which may help in developing machine learning models to track public narratives using artificial intelligence.

A square or rectangle represents one of our collection of aricles, with consistent visual semantics through the following visualization.

The color block represents the narrative disadvantage content of the article being coded, which warm color for groups and cold color for issues metioned in this article.

Factiva search returned n=3845 documents that included that search terms.

We searched Factiva using the same set of terms that were developed for the review (e.g. disadvantage* OR discrimination OR "access to resources" …)
We included the most-read newspaper in every state and territory, plus The Australian (top national newspaper), Daily Telegraph (second in NSW) and Herald Sun (second in Vic)
We restricted the searches to Commentaries/Opinions Or Editorials published in 2020.

The top 1000 most 'Relecant' article were downloaded and randomised for screening and coding.

Human coders screened articles (n=588)

And extracted data from those that met inclusion criteria (n=265), then coding stopped.

Opinion pieces will be the focus of this study because they: (1) are likely to contain more narrative content (as opposed to the factual content that is generally presented in news stories); (2) typically highlight salient issues that are the forefront of public debate (Golan, 2010); (3) can impact public opinion and voting behaviour (Dalton et al., 1998; Druckman & Parkin, 2005; (4) are available in volumes that  are more amenable to human coding than other larger datasets, such as factual news stories; and, (5) were considered to be high-priority by the expert advisory panel.
NOTE: the 265 number is only for this visualization show case, not the real project.
